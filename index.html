The three most common temperature scales are Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), and Kelvin (K). Celsius is widely used in most countries and in scientific calculations, while Fahrenheit is primarily used in the United States. Kelvin is the SI unit of temperature and is commonly used in scientific fields.

Temperature conversions between these scales are as follows:

  • Celsius to Fahrenheit: (°C × 9/5) + 32 = °F
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius: (°F - 32) × 5/9 = °C
  • Celsius to Kelvin: °C + 273.15 = K
  • Kelvin to Celsius: K - 273.15 = °C
  • Fahrenheit to Kelvin: (°F - 32) × 5/9 + 273.15 = K
  • Kelvin to Fahrenheit: (K - 273.15) × 9/5 + 32 = °F
